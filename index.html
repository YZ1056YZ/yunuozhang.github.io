About Me

I am a Computer Science Ph.D. candidate at Vanderbilt University, working under the guidance of Dr. Abhishek Dubey. My research focuses on developing robust algorithms for sequential decision-making under uncertainty, with particular emphasis on belief representation methods for partially observable Markov decision processes (POMDPs) and adaptive planning in non-stationary environments.

My research interests span three key areas: Sequential Decision-Making Under Partial Observability, where I develop advanced algorithms for POMDPs including particle-based belief representation, annealed importance sampling, and Monte Carlo tree search with applications to real-time planning under uncertainty; Adaptive Planning in Non-Stationary Environments, focusing on online learning and planning methods that adapt to changing environmental dynamics, including in-context learning with temporal abstractions and policy-augmented search for evolving systems; and AI for Time-Critical Applications, deploying decision-theoretic algorithms to safety-critical domains including UAV search and rescue operations and smart grid vehicle-to-building systems.

I have published first-author papers at premier venues including NeurIPS, ICAPS, and AAMAS, advancing both theoretical foundations and practical applications of decision-making under uncertainty. Through industry collaborations, I have worked with DARPA's ANSR program on UAV search and rescue in urban environments, and partnered with Nissan Motor Corporation on vehicle-to-building energy optimization using real-world EV data.

Currently, I am particularly interested in using particle-based approaches to approximate high-dimensional and multi-modal posterior distributions, addressing fundamental challenges in belief representation for complex real-world systems where traditional parametric methods fail to capture the intricate uncertainty structures.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I earned my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science and Applied Mathematics from Vanderbilt University, where I developed a strong foundation in both theoretical computer science and mathematical optimization that underpins my current research.

Research Overview

My research focuses on developing robust algorithms for sequential decision-making under uncertainty, particularly in partially observable and non-stationary environments. I design novel belief representation methods and adaptive planning algorithms that enable autonomous agents to maintain accurate probabilistic models and make optimal decisions despite limited observability and environmental changes.

My work spans theoretical contributions in POMDPs and Monte Carlo tree search to practical applications in autonomous systems, emergency response, and energy management.
